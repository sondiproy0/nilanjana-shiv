"চা"(Tea)

Growing up in Bangladesh, where tea (চা- cha) is more than a beverage; it's a way of life. Here, the daily rhythm revolves around "cha", as we affectionately call it. The bustling tea stalls, or "chaer tong", are ubiquitous, offering a respite from the sweltering heat and a backdrop for heartfelt conversations.

The love for tea runs deep, with rituals woven into the social fabric, from the roadside tea vendors to traditional tea ceremonies at home. The "dudh cha" (milk tea) infused with spices or the aromatic "rong cha" (green tea/ lemon tea) is an integral part of every gathering, be it celebratory or somber. It's not just about the taste; it's about the camaraderie, the sharing of stories, and the connections forged over countless cups. In Bangladesh, tea is not just a beverage; it's a bridge that unites people, a comfort that transcends borders, and a symbol of hospitality that warms both hearts and homes.

Motivated by the warmth, camaraderie, and diverse flavors, I yearn to master the craft of brewing all the various kinds of teas representing our culture turning every sip into a shared experience.
